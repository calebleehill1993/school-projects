Heirloom Baby Blanket

Pattern- Pretty Fans and Feathers from the book “Our Best Knit Baby Afghans”
Yarn- Drops Puna 100% Alpaca

After trying to conceive for many years my oldest sister finally became pregnant with her miracle baby. When she did, she asked if I would knit a baby blanket for him, and any future children they have, to be blessed in. Honored, I set about sending her patterns and possible yarn options to approve. She settled on a 100% alpaca yarn, and the Pretty Fans and Feathers pattern.

I was hesitant to do a baby blanket out of 100% alpaca, as it is an expensive yarn that must be hand washed. Not exactly baby friendly. However, my sister assured me that this would be a strictly ceremonial heirloom blanket that would be used once for each of her children, then put in a box or displayed as a treasured keepsake. Usage would be rare enough that the issue of handwashing the blanket as necessary didn’t bother her.

Knitting in process

And so, I began the process. Knitting with alpaca is always such a pleasure! The first thing I ever knitted was an alpaca scarf. I was spoiled as a young knitter due to the fact I worked at a shop that had started out as a yarn store. Whenever I get the chance to knit with alpaca, which is not often anymore, it makes me happy!

The final product was soft and beautiful! I had so much fun working with the alpaca yarn, I used an extra skein to also make a pair of booties and a hat to match the blanket! You can check out those projects here:
